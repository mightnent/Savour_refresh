The Ultimate List of Summarised Circuit Breaker Support for F&B Businesses [Updated Regularly]

Last updated: 01 May 2020


Feeling overwhelmed by all the information on the various F&B support coming in every day scattered all over your social media feed?

Team at Savour! have researched, compiled and summarised the latest essential information going around social media relating to the key F&B support from the government, private companies, charity groups, and individuals offered during the circuit breaker period.

Namely, delivery, funding, and marketing support for local F&B businesses and how local F&B businesses can help the needy.

We will be updating new information in this article regularly. We welcome community contributions so if you have any relevant information to share with us, please fill up a short Google form in the link HERE then we will review and add your update into this article accordingly.

Funding support for local F&B businesses

  1. Enterprise Singapore — Food Delivery Booster Package
  • About: Offset 5% of the commissions charged by Deliveroo, GrabFood and Foodpandafor orders delivered between April 7 and May 4. Save 20% on your delivery costs if youfulfil orders through third-party logistics firms
  • Apply now at Enterprise Singapore's Website
  1. ChopeAndSave
  • About: A website saving F&B and many other businesses by listing gift cards to purchase to use after the circuit breaker measures are lifted 
  • Sell a gift card there now
  1. LALAMOVE - Delivery Booster Package for F&B Businesses
  • About: Available to F&B businesses fulfilling food delivery orders through Lalamove. ESG will fund 20% of the delivery cost per trip for orders made between 7 April and 4 May 2020. No cap on the qualifying food delivery cost. From 7 April through to 4 May 2020, all delivery transactions processed will receive a 20% rebate. To be eligible, F&B businesses must have a food shop or food stall license issued by SFA, and sell food prepared on-premise for immediate consumption.
  • Submit an application here
  1. National Environment Agency
  • About: Hawkers operating at hawker centres managed by NEA or NEA-appointed operators who sign up to food delivery platforms and third-party logistics will get one-time funding of $500 to offset delivery costs incurred to deliver food between April 7 and May 31. Those who have signed up before April 7 can also receive the funding. To receive the funding, stallholders can submit documentation of their agreement with the delivery platform, along with proof of food delivery orders made between April 7 and May 31 to nea_hpg@nea.gov.sg. Upon verification, $500 will be credited to the stallholder's bank account.
  • Drop an email to NEA
  1. Nas Daily - Move Online Fund
  • About: Nas Daily put in the first $10,000 into a fund to help small Singaporean businesses like mom & pop shops to move online by building them a website, subsidizing their delivery costs, helping them advertise or whatever they need.To apply, simply visit the website and include details about your business, as well as the challenges you face to shift operations online.
  • Apply on the website now
  1. Damn Worth It - Provide service to help 15 merchants to set up own website
  • About: Damn Worth It will ensure it costs the merchant less than $125 to set their own website up and be tagging the merchant to a reliable logistic partner. Contact +65 97663115 (Canny) to request for the service now. 
  • View Facebook post for more details
