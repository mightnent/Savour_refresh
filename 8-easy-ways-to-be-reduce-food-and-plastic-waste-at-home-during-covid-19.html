8 Easy Ways To Reduce Food And Plastic waste at home during covid19


How has your past one month of circuit breaker period been?

âœ‹ Hands up if you are spending more time whipping up more insta-worthy dishes in the kitchen. However, with many items being out of stock in supermarkets as well as the price increases of many items, the way we buy food has changed drastically and so has the way we inevitably incur food wastage.

With the recent extension of circuit breaker, the amount of food and plastic waste we incur at home would continue to increase exponentially as households and F&B businesses adjust the way people cook and consume. Since 2019, Singapore has been working towards becoming a zero-waste nation.

So, read on below to learn more about what are some small steps you can take to play your part to help Singapore become a zero-waste nation!ðŸ‘‡

  1. Be more conscious of the amount of perishable food items like fresh fruits and vegetables that you purchase by simply checking what you have left in the fridge before heading out for grocery shopping.

  2. Freeze your leftovers and double check that it is still safe for consumption the next time you want to consume it instead of merely throwing it away.

  3. Use the ‘best before date’ labelled on food products as a guide. If it looks and tastes alright, it probably is okay for consumption. (Do check out our previous blogpost regarding the difference between expiry and best before dates!)

  4. Plan your meals a few days or a week ahead so that you roughly know the amount of ingredients that you will need then purchase just enough for that.

  5. Stop peeling vegetables that don’t need to be peeled such as carrots, cucumber and turnips! In fact, not peeling some of these vegetables, comes with benefits as well.

  6. Calm down while shopping for groceries, and not be a hoarder!

  7. Bring along reusable containers and bottles when you go to buy food or drinks. However, when choosing what type of takeaway container to bring along, do keep in mind that:
    - Stainless steel: Great for takeaways, however note that is not microwaveable.
    - Glass: Great, but beware of glass’ brittle nature!
    - Plastic: Avoid bringing plastic containers for taking away hot food as high temperatures can melt plastic!

8. When shopping for groceries, do remember to bring along reusable bags or you can purchase a foldable trolley to store your groceries too!

The circuit breaker period has highlighted the importance of taking care of one another in the community (shoutout to Goodhood.sg, a mobile app that connects neighbours to do good in the ‘hood, which has really stepped up during Covid-19 and has since helped many Singaporeans) but let us not forget that it is of paramount importance to take care of our environment that we all live in.

If everyone puts in a little effort to be more environmentally sustainable by incorporating these easy and simple habits into their lives, we can make the world a better place for us all, one less plastic item and food item wasted at a time.ðŸ˜„
